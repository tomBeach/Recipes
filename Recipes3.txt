Columbian Coconut Chicken and Rice

ingredients
4 1/2 cups shredded coconut (divided)
2 T grapeseed oil
1 t ground turmeric
1 medium yellow onion (finely chopped)
8 garlic cloves
1 T tomato paste
1 1/2 t ground allspice
1 T soysauce
2 lbs boneless/skinless chicken thighs
1 T lime juice
2 t lime zest
1 pt grape tomatos (halved)
1 cup unsweetened coconut milk (preferably full fat)
1 1/2 cups long-grain white rice
2 t salt

instructions
• In blender combine 2 1/4 cups coconut and 3 cups warm water
• Let stand until coconut begins to soften (1 minute)
• Continue blending until on high (1-2 minutes), then strain to remove larger solids
• Repeat with remaining 2 cups of coconut.

• Heat oil till shimmering in large dutch oven, then add turmeric and cook until fragrant (30 seconds)
• Add onion and garlic.  Cook and stir until onion softens (3 minutes)
• Stir in tomato paste and allspice, then 1 1/2 cups of coconut milk, soy and 1 teaspoon salt
• Bring to simmer, then add chicken, cover, and reduce to medium-low.
• Cook until inserted skewer meets no resistance (18-22 minutes)
• NOTE: flip chicken pieces after 10 minutes

• In a medium saucerpan on high heat, stir together remaining 3 cups coconut milk, rice and 2 teaspoons of salt.  Bring to a simmer, cover, reduce heat to low and cook until liquid is absorbed (15 minutes)
• Remove pan from heat and let stand uncovered for 5 minutes.
• Fluff rice to fold in coconut oil, then remove meat and bring liquid to a simmer to reduce volume by half (12 minutes) while stirring.
• Remove from heat and stir in lime zest, lime juice and tomatos.  Return chicken to pot, heat to serving temperature and serve.




Ropa Vieja

ingredients:

1 1/2 lbs flank steak
salt and pepper
1 1/2 tbsp cumin seeds
1 1/2 tbsp coriander seeds
1 tbsp olive oil
1 medium white onion (thinly sliced lengthwise)
1 red bell pepper
1 jalapeno chili (sliced into thin rounds)
5 medium garlic cloves
14 oz can whole peeled tomatoes
1/2 cup beef broth
2 carrots (chopped)
1 1/2 tbsp lime juice
lime wedges (for serving)

instructions:

• Preheat oven to 350 degrees
• Season meat on both sides with salt and pepper
• Toast cumin and coriander seeds until fragrant in dutch oven (1-3 min)
• Grind in spice grinder after cooling
• Over medium high heat stir in oil, onions, bell pepper, jalapeno, garlic with spices.  Add 3/4 tsp salt.
• Cover and cook, stirring occasionally (8-10 min)
• Stir in tomatoes (including juice) and cook until most liquid evaporates (5-7 min)
• Nestle meat into vegetables, then add broth, cover, and bake for 2 hours
• Uncover pot and add chopped carrots
• Continue cooking until paring knife can be inserted with no resistance (about 1 hour)
• Remove from oven and cool for 20 min
• Use kitchen shears to snip meat into 1 inch strips
• Stir in lime juice; season with salt and pepper as needed
• Serve with lime wedges







Oaxacan Mole with Chicken

ingredients:

2 pounds boneless/skinless thighs
7 6-inch tortillas
1 qt chix broth
2 medium tomatillos
1 poblano pepper
1 white onion
4 cloves garlic
1 cup flat leaf parsley
1 bunch cilantro
8 oz small golden potatoes
6 oz green beans
1/2 cup mint
1 tsp fennel seed
1 tsp cumin
salt
pepper

instructions:

• Season chicken thighs with salt and pepper.
• In large pot over medium-high heat combine tortillas and broth.  Bring to boil then transfer tortillas to blender.
• Add 1/4 cup water and blend until smooth (1 minute).
• Pour puree into boiling broth.
• Add chicken to pot, cover and reduce heat to low.  Simmer 30-35 minutes.
• Heat broiler with rack 4" from top.  Line baking sheet with foil and place garlic, tomatillos, poblano chili and onion on baking sheet.
• Broil until slightly charred (about 4 minutes) then flip and broil 3-5 minutes more.
• Cool then transfer to blender.
• Add 1/2 cup water then puree until smooth (1/2 minute).  Add cilantro, parsely, mint, fennel, cumin, salt and pepper.  Blend until smooth and bright green (2 minutes).
• Add potatoes, green beans, and zucchini to pot.  Simmer over medium heat uncovered (about 15 minutes).
• Season with salt and pepper.





 One-Pan Chicken and Lentils

ingredients:

1 large onion
2-inch piece fresh ginger root
2 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil
1 1/2 cups dried red lentils
One 14.5- or 15-ounce can low-fat coconut milk (see NOTE)
1 1/2 cups no-salt-added or homemade chicken broth
2 teaspoons chili powder
About 2 pounds bone-in chicken thighs
Kosher salt
Freshly ground black pepper
1/4 cup plain yogurt (full-fat or low-fat)
1 teaspoon ground cumin
3 tomatoes from one 14.5-ounce can whole peeled tomatoes
Lemon, for serving

instructions:

• Position a rack in the upper third of the oven; preheat to 425 degrees.
• Cut the onion into 1/2-inch dice.
• Peel the ginger, then mince it or grate it against the large-holed side of a box grater.
• Toss together the onion, ginger and oil in a 9-by-13-inch metal roasting pan, until coated. Roast for 8 minutes, then remove from the oven.
• Meanwhile, rinse the lentils under cool running water and drain.
• Stir the coconut milk, broth and 1 1/2 teaspoons of the chili powder into the onion mixture in the roasting pan, then stir in the lentils.
• Cover tightly with aluminum foil and roast for 8 minutes.
• While the lentils are in the oven, pat dry the chicken (first discard the skin as needed), then season the meat generously with salt and pepper.
• Stir together the yogurt, cumin and the remaining 1/2 teaspoon of chili powder in a bowl, then coat the chicken thighs with that mixture.
• Cut the canned tomatoes into chunks.
• Remove the roasting pan from the oven, then uncover and stir in the tomatoes.
• Season the pan mixture lightly with salt and pepper.
• Nestle the coated chicken thighs into the lentil-tomato mixture, skinned sides up.
• Return the pan to the oven (uncovered); roast for 20 minutes, so the chicken is cooked through and lightly browned in spots.
• Cut the lemon into wedges. Serve warm, with the lemon.

NOTE: Transfer the contents of canned coconut milk to a larger container or canister designed for an immersion (stick) blender, making sure to scrape all the solidified coconut cream/fat from the can. Puree until emulsified (even a little frothy). Or you can use a whisk.

 Pappardelle With Beef Ragu

ingredients
1 3/4 pounds boneless beef chuck roast, in 2-inch cubes
1  tablespoon extra virgin olive oil
2  sprigs rosemary, plus 1 tablespoon finely chopped leaves for garnish
2  sprigs sage
1  small red onion, peeled and cut in chunks
4  cloves garlic, finely chopped
1  carrot, peeled and thickly sliced
1  celery stalk, thickly sliced
2  cups Chianti
1  28-ounce can peeled whole cherry or plum tomatoes
1  pound pappardelle
3  tablespoons butter
1  tablespoon finely grated orange zest
½  cup freshly grated Parmigiano-Reggiano
Salt and freshly ground black pepper


instructions
• Season beef with salt and pepper to taste.
• Place an uncovered pressure cooker or oven-proof Dutch oven over medium-high heat, and add olive oil. When oil is hot, add beef.
• Stir until beef is well browned on all sides, about 5 minutes.
• Add rosemary and sage sprigs, onion, garlic, carrot and celery. Reduce heat to medium-low and sauté until vegetables are softened, about 5 minutes.

• Add Chianti and continue to simmer until liquid has reduced by half, about 15 minutes.
• Add tomatoes and their juices.
• Using Dutch oven simmer on the stove over low heat, for 3 to 3 1/2 hours.

• Place a large pot of lightly salted water over high heat to bring to a boil.
• Add pappardelle to boiling water.
• Remove Dutch oven from oven.
• Using two forks, finely shred meat and vegetables. Discard herb stems.
• Loosely cover pan and return it to low heat to keep warm.
• As it cooks, scoop out 1/2 cup water and reserve.
• Cook pasta to taste, then drain well.
• Return pasta to pot, and add butter and 1/4 cup Parmigiano-Reggiano; mix gently until butter has melted.
• Add a little reserved cooking water to loosen.

To serve, lift pasta into each of six shallow bowls. Spoon beef ragù over top. Sprinkle each bowl with a pinch of orange zest and rosemary, and a spoonful of cheese.



Orange-Guajillo Pulled Chicken

ingredients
5 medium 	guajillo chillies (stemmed, seeded, torn into pieces)
1 1/2 cups 	orange juice
1/2	medium onion, roughly chopped
2 T	olive oil
5 cloves	garlic
2 T	white vinegar
2 t ground 	coriander
2 t	honey
1 t 	dried oregano
1 t 	salt
2 lbs 	boneless/skinless thighs

instructions
• In a large skillet over medium-high heat toast chillies (press with spatula) about 1 min (flip after 30 secs)
• Transfer to bowl and add orange juice. Let stand until softened (10 min)
• Saute onion briefly in olive oil
• Transfer juice mixture to blender and add garlic, vinegar, coriander, honey, oregano and salt.  Puree until smooth (30 secs)
• Pour into skillet and bring to boil
• Add chicken thighs and cover.  Cook over low-medium heat until tender (20 min)
• Set chicken on plate, cool, then shred into pieces
• Simmer sauce over medium-high heat until reduced to 1 cup (10 min)
• Stir chicken into sauce, season with salt and pepper



Stir-Fried Chicken With Lime and Coconut

ingredients
4 boneless, skinless chicken breast halves, cut into bite-size pieces
2 limes (grated zest and juice)
4 teaspoons olive oil
2 jalapeño peppers, seeded and finely chopped
1 cup unsweetened coconut milk (preferably full fat)
4 teaspoons fish sauce
6 to 8 scallions, both white and light-green parts, cut into 1-inch shreds
1/2 cup chopped cilantro

instructions
• In a medium bowl, combine the chicken pieces with the lime zest and juice. Let sit for 15 minutes (at room temperature) or in the refrigerator for up to 1 hour.
• Heat the oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
• Drain the chicken and pat the pieces dry
• Once the oil is shimmering, add the chicken and cook for 3 to 4 minutes, stirring frequently, or until the chicken is golden.
• Add the jalapeños and stir-fry for 1 minute more.
• Add the coconut milk, fish sauce, half the scallions and half the cilantro.
• Cook for 1 to 2 minutes more.
• Sprinkle with the remaining scallions and cilantro and serve.
